ISO 9001 to ULQF Crosswalk

This crosswalk maps selected ISO 9001 concepts to ULQF constructs used in lunar and autonomous operations. It serves as a navigation aid for reviewers and partners, not a certification guide.

Download: ISO9001_ULQF_Crosswalk.csv

ISO 9001 clause Intent ULQF mapping Notes for lunar or delayed ops
4.4 QMS and its processes Define, sequence, and control core processes ULQF quality architecture and process authority structure Distributed ops require explicit authority handoffs and verification windows
6.1 Actions to address risks and opportunities Plan controls for uncertainty and change ULQF risk governance and hazard drift controls Delay increases evidence aging, so model decision windows and triggers
7.1.5 Monitoring and measuring resources Ensure measurement capability and validity ULQF metrology validity, sensor drift detection, evidence confidence Thermal cycling and dust create drift; treat confidence decay as a control variable
8.5 Production and service provision Execute under controlled conditions ULQF CTQ control, verification load planning, process drift monitoring Autonomy requires verified control modes and off-nominal transitions
8.7 Control of nonconforming outputs Contain and disposition nonconformance ULQF anomaly containment, quarantine logic, mission risk disposition Remote disposition must include latency-aware escalation and safe states
9.1 Monitoring, measurement, analysis and evaluation Measure performance and decision inputs ULQF data integrity, decision fidelity metrics, leading indicators Track data age and confidence, not just accuracy, under delayed telemetry

Note: This mapping is descriptive. ISO and ULQF terms are not interchangeable, and the crosswalk does not imply certification, endorsement, or compliance claims.
